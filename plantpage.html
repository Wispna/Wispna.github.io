Plants

A variety of useful plants have sprouted up in the botanical garden! For your character to recognize them, they must roll a lore of one. Alternatively, if your character has worked in an apothecary in their background, they will be able to recognize the apothecary plants.

General Plants



The giant tree that grows in the center of the botanical gardens. Its branches are laden with golden fruit, and each fruit glows with a soft light. It’s said that as long as it’s well taken care of, its presence strengthens the growth of other plants.

The golden fruit that grows on the giant tree in the center of botanical gardens. The fruit glows with a soft golden light, and when plucked, will glow for 12 hours when before fading. It will glow indefinitely if regularly refreshed with Nature Creation magic. When eaten, it has a light sweet taste.

Tiny red berries that are incredibly spicy. When not used as spice, it can also dye cloth yellow if steeped with cloth for a short time, or orange if steeped with cloth for a long time, though be careful not to burn your hands when using it.

Adora's favorite plant, this rose with a pink center and white outside symbolizes love and purity. It can dye cloth pink with its outer petals, and red with its inner petals. Otherwise, it’s just a universal message of love.

A leafy plant with wide, sour leaves. Digging it up reveals a purplish root. The root can be eaten on its own, or its juices can be used to flavor things and make them sweet. Alternatively the root can be used to dye cloth purple, while the leaves can be used to dye things green.

In the quiet of a moonless night, sometimes you can hear a low chime from the flower known as the nightbelles. One of these blooms in your room is said to give you good dreams for the night. Alternatively, night belles can be used as a blue dye.

This fruit is one of the juiciest and most delicious fruits, however, it's said to cause terrible gas, stinkiness, and odors.

This flower is popular in luxury perfumes due to its seductive scent. It is said a person is more attractive when they smell like this flower.

​Crushing these blossoms into a tea will temporarily relieve you of your sorrows. Too much ingestion results in dependency, and will result in hard crashes of depression when withdrawn from them. They constantly look like they’re wet with dew.

A plant that can hold a fire for 5 hours when lit. Things that could normally douse a campfire can still douse the flame it holds.

Little yellow berries that grow on a bush. They’re sweet, and often used on pastries.

An herb with a pleasant soapy smell and a savory taste. An excellent hair rinse can be made with Naier, and myths say if you place Naier under your pillow you’ll remember your dreams.

This small, white round fruit grows from bushes. The fruits would look almost like eggs, if not for the small crystals that seem to sprout from its surface. These crystals are actually chunks of salt that can be harvested and used on foods. The fruit itself can also be eaten, but it’s quite salty, has a tough texture, and is in general rather unpleasant to eat on its own.

Woody nuts seem to grow from this plant, but when opened, soft balls of pale fibers can be found within. The fibers come in whites, off whites, pale greens, and pale browns, and can be woven into thread with a spinning wheel, then woven into cloth on a loom.

Certain virtuous people put the bloom of this flower on their tongue to stave off lust.

The branches sag from the bountiful fruit.



Apothecary Plants

Note: Plants marked with an asterisk* require the apothecary to be completed before certain effects can be used.



A useful flower, that when mashed into a pulp, mixed with water, and applied to a wound it can prevent infection. It can also numb some pain for an hour or so. It's said Alorel herself invented the flower in an act of mercy.

This plant is toxic, but when distilled with tools from a completed apothecary, it can cure any wound as long as the substance is poured directly on the wound, and as long as the recipient has a night’s rest.

This blue flower was said to have been created when a unicorn touched its horn to a common weed. When distilled with tools from a completed apothecary, it can act as the antidote for any poison. The antidote must be used right away, as the effectiveness fades an hour after distillation. There are only a few blooms, however, so it’s best not to pick them all at once without getting a nature mage to refresh their numbers. ​

This spiked red berry looks unpleasant, but when peeled, and boiled with juice from raplin root, it can be set out to harden into a lozenge that when sucked on is quite good at soothing sore throats and coughs.

This plant has a garlicky taste, and an unpleasant sour smell. It can give you quite the stomach ache when eaten, but is believed to have the power to treat a wide variety of diseases far worse than a stomach ache. Not all the diseases it can treat are known, but when someone has an infected wound, or a sore throat that won’t let up, Belgo is always issued as a treatment.

This pleasant plant can commonly be seen throughout Tiranthe, and has little pink blooms. It’s more effective before it has bloomed, however, and if you eat the leaves, or brew the leaves into a tea, it is said to ward off fever, and treat headaches.

Insects dislike the oils this plant produces, and when ingested, it’s said spicori can rid you of internal parasites.

A pretty orange flower that can be brewed into a calming tea. When dried before being brewed, it can also act as a sleep aid. Some are resistant to its properties, however, much to the chagrin of poor sleepers.

The leaves of this bush produce a pungent oil. When the leaves are steamed, and the steam is breathed in, it’s said to ease breathing problems. When dried, and placed in a sachet, some say it acts as a charm to ward off evil. It can also be used to remove dye.

Many travelers know about noro noro fruit, particularly in its dried form. It was originally a popular travel food in Dethi, but quickly spread to other regions due to its ability to grow in great multitudes in noro noro trees, and due to its nutritional value, and its filling nature. It's said that one can live on noro noro fruit and water alone. When dried, noro noro fruit can last for months, and still retain its healthful benefits. Despite this, many still turn their nose up at eating the fruit due to its incredibly bitter taste

A soft and creamy fruit inside with a spikey shell thats on fire. The 'fire' is just visible during sunny days as it is just a gas reacting to the suns rays. It is said that this fruit will divide people into either loving it or hating it. Roll 1d100 to see which one you are. below 69 you love the taste of this fruit. Above 69 and you hate it. When you roll 69 burian gies you a really good dream or the best bathroom break ever.

A plant indigenous to Lealia that can grow to be many meters tall. It's carnivorous, emits a sweet scent, and delicious looking red fruits grow along its massive green stem. It's head is usually closed in a bud, but when it's ready to eat, the head will split apart to reveal a floral shape filled with teeth. The Myrenn botanical garden cares for one of these plants.

A plant that blooms only every eight years. In nature it grows near minerals that have strong magic absorption qualities, and at day it absorbs sunlight, while at night it absorbs excess magic. When it blooms it starts release large amounts of magic to attract mageflies which are its primary pollinator. Its nectar tastes quite good, and the Myrenn botanical garden will host a viewing when it's time for the adora passiflora to bloom

A small green plant with handlike leaves. It grows on a small island near Scatha, and nowhere else, and is one of the rarest plants in Tiranthe. It works as an emetic, but isn't often used for such purposes.

A vibrant red plant with purple stripes. It grows at the edge of Vieru. When immersed in water, and sprayed on someone it can kill them quickly, but it turns sections of their skin a vibrant purple. It was one of the many weapons used in the Andaran war.
