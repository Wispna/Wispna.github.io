Ishari

Region Name: Ishari

Capital City: Shen, The Emerald Jewel

Primary God: Bao-rha

Races: Xanthi 80%, Humans, 10%, Everything else 10%


Description: Ishari is a region primarily composed of grasslands and forests. There is no central governing body, but the region is entirely composed of separate clans. The leaders from each major clan hold a summit annually in the capital city of Shen. Shen is a neutral zone where no weapons or violence is allowed. They take turns leading the summit annually so there is a sense of equal opportunity. There are many smaller clans as well that are constantly trying to gain a seat at the summit in a grab for power and legitimacy.

Secondary

How the rest of Tiranthe views them: They are seen as not a big threat because they don’t have a central government and cannot organize. They have strong warriors, but no official standing army.

Issues this country faces: In-fighting between clans and clan leaders trying to gain prominence. Strong traditional values.

What’s the economy like?: It’s not particularly prosperous, nor particularly poor. Education is not standardized as it’s usually taught by elders in clans.

What are the main languages spoken?: There are a variety of obscure Xanthi languages only known to certain clans, but most Xanthi know the common tongue.

Where do most people live?: In disparate clans in the plains, but also by the seaside in seaside disparate clans.

How does this country view magic?: With slight distaste as they value physical prowess and ability foremost.

How technologically advanced is this country? Is magic preferred?: Not very technologically advanced as they prefer to stick to traditional means that have been passed down through the generations. Magic is generally distrusted.

How is history recorded?: Through oral language and stories.

How many are educated, and how?: They are mostly educated in combat.

How did the majority of current people come to be on this land?: They have lived here for generations.

Why do people choose to live here?: Most people don’t unless they wish to live a more pastoral lifestyle or to improve their physical combat/ability.

How popular is the government?: Having your clan be part of the annual council comes with great prestige and elevates the status of a clan.

How are criminals charged and punished?: Trial by combat. If you do not die from the combat, you are spiritually dead to the clan. Everyone ignores you and burns your stuff. Ouch. Those that are ostracized often die of sadness or move to other regions. Lots of sad mercenaries.
