Stats:(18 Stat points to start. You must have at least 1 point in VIT, or your character is dead? Each stat is capped at 8 max.)

VIT - Every point in Vitality is essentially how many successes you can take before you go down. One point of Vitality is one point of HP.

PHYS - This deals with traditional feats of the human body, such as demonstrations of strength, speed, and nimbleness.

SORC - Magical Ability.

ACA - An educational knowledge. Academics covers history, politics, geography, understanding technology and creating it, economics, popular religious customs, the ins and outs of magical theory, and investigation.

LORE - A more traditional knowledge. Lore covers stories, songs, useful plants, creatures that roam the world, customs of places you've traveled, perception, and you have more of a hold on rumors. Maybe someone with high Lore doesn't know all the religious custom and rituals, but they'll know of the godly myths.

BLESS - Occasional aid from your god/goddess. Luck, in a way.

Stats:

Our combat system relies on rolling 6-sided dice with 5s and 6s as successes. The number of dice you are allowed to roll for a trait depends on the number of Stat points you have in them.

Someone with 5 stat points in physical traits will be allowed to roll 5 dice when making a physical attack or physical move.

If you want to make a physically based character, you may prefer higher traits in PHYS, if you are making a magically inclined character, you may prefer higher traits in SORC, and if you prefer a mixture you might want to split the difference.

Allocate your 18 points however you would like. You can even have 0 points in any trait except vitality if you would like.



Choose your Combat Type:

You can now choose your character’s ability traits. You can go full physical, full magical, or any mix in-between. These abilities do have mechanical features in-game, but they can also have roleplay features and do not necessarily have to be used for combat. All these abilities will cost Attribution Points. For full details, please refer to the Physical Tree and Magic System docs.

All Ability types are further stratified between Minor, Moderate, and Major abilities. You may not obtain a Major ability before obtaining its Minor and Moderate.

Minor -> Moderate -> Major

If you wish to specialize in multiple ability types, you must first master at least 1 Major ability in an ability type before learning a new one.

The physical and magical types are also organized in a circle. The further a type is from another, the more attribution points it will cost to learn an ability from the other type due to the extremely different or clashing natures.

Someone who is learning the MIGHTY path (red) will have greater difficulty learning from the DEFT path (green) due to the very different natures of the two/distance on the circle and therefore it will cost them more Attribution Points.

Someone who is on the LIGHTNING path (yellow) would have greater difficulty learning the MINERAL path (brown), shown in the distance on their circle/path, and thus it would cost them more Attribution Points.

At level 1, you have 30 attribution points to allocate between physical or magical traits or both. Some races will have access to certain magical types that others do not. For more details, please refer to the RACES page.
