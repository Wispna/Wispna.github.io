Approaching the solarium, a rather imposing tent has set itself up right upon the steps to the grand, mystical building. The tent is filled with lavish reds and pinks, flowing together in a dance of swirling colors. It's nearly disorienting just looking at it, and yet, the warm tones are rather inviting. Upon entering these curtains you find yourself washed in the colors as they bleed through the thin fabric guarding the inside from the sunlight. Your skin is covered in these colors, and the warmth of the inside fills you with comfort. You feel as if you could stay awhile! A single chair sits in the middle of the dirt floor of the tent. It's old, rickety, and made mostly of wood, but it has been remade, red minerals encircling cracks and misshapen pieces from years of negligence. It's rather comfortable, with a large, plush, off-white fur draped over the back and seat of the chair, so it's not terribly uncomfortable. Three curtains, each with their own invisible partitions sit facing the chair. Each is a different color, one red, one blue, one green. These curtains are made of a much thicker fabric than the ones separating the rest of the festival from the inside, not even a silhouette of whoever is behind them could be seen. They lie in wait, with a sign in front, written rather elegantly. "Tell us the woes of your heart, and wisdom we shall impart."
